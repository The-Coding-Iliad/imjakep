Major Contributions
  • MuleSoft Implementation

Within 18 months of joining Vice Media the team whom I Project Managed in Wrike, had completely replaced the previous Dell Boomi application with MuleSoft Middleware. This Middleware connected our ERP system (NetSuite) with our expense reports application Concur, our vendor payment system Coupa, our CRM SalesForce, and a in-house developed application for Video Tagging called Service Projects. Despite the high turn-over rate, we were able to map all data and execute their configurations within a mix of real-time and batched API's. Thanks to MuleSoft's ability to recicyle the use of it's API's this will change the way data is passed throughout Vice Media Group, and it will continue to serve the organization long into the future, as Vice realizes its goal to transition from a point-to-point integration structure to a hub-and-spoke data web.

  • In House Application Development

As my first true SDLC project that I managed in Jira with a team of Front-End, Back-End & Full-Stack Developers, this was a fantastic oppurtunity to wet my appetite to learn more about coding. The application was meant amalgamate information from disparate system reports into a single website with an easily navigable UI for users to create their own custom reports, without needing to download these individual reports and collate them in excel files. Seeing them run through their sprints and complete pull requests with a smile and seeing how they could work independantly on their own tasks and still come together and help each other is what truly inspired me to learn Full-Stack.

  • NetSuite Projects

Alot of firsts for me in this company, as this was also my first time working with an ERP system! I rolled up my sleeves and helped the NetSuite team complete projects such as the configuration of Budget Code Line Items, Deferred Expense Release Module, 1099 year end reporting among others. As a project based organization, meaning all revenue and expenses are allocated to projects managed in NetSuite, Vice had a very customized environment which proved to be a challenge in its maintenance. I not only helped them complete their projects, but also brought a sense of transperency and stability as they was a lot of churn within the team

Major Contribution
  • Business Requirements Document: EHR

Imagine lil' ol me walking into a park avenue high rise office building in the best off the rack suit I could find, suffering from imposter syndrome among the high profile muckity-mucks. Despite this, my short term contract with the NFL gave me the oppurtunity to have a lunch meeting with the countries largest Ambulatory EHR software provider, flew me around the country to attend NFL conferences, and had me going to locker rooms to meet with athletic trainers. This was EHR on a different level. A completely custom source code with its own unique team of developers and support staff created solely for the NFL. It was my job to not only make sure it's use continued with minimal problems, but also to collect and compose requirements to be developed for the upcoming 2019 season. Stakeholders in this environment were not playing on the same team, and this is not just a figure of speech. Workarounds, were often considered competitive advantage so consensus building was paramount. By the end of the 2018 season, an extensive backlog was curated compiled into a BRD and passed onto the development team at e-Clinical Works.

Major Contributions
  • Onboarding the Mental Health Department

An entire Line of Business was performing their work on paper, just as they had always done, just as the most of world did until the HITECH Act made it too lucrative to not use an EHR. I was tasked with bringing them onto the enterprise EHR within 6 months. I worked closely with end users gathering requirements, I worked independently to develop the system to match these requirements, I worked closely with the executive team to keep them apprised of the status via dashboards created on Google Workspace, and lastly i worked closely with the end users as I provided them training and Go - Live support. This project is what helped me pivot from closing tickets as an analyst to functioning as a high performing project manager. This was the initial success that would catapult my EHR career.

  • Grant Program Management

Rarely in the IT space do we have the oppurtunity to generate revenue directly. We are often considered cost centers, which enable the rest of the business to function. Not the case when the organization is obtaining over $500K in grant revenue as a direct result of your use of the EHR system. The organization would seek out both federal and state grant money, and then task me with changing workflows in the system and delivering reports on this data to obtain these grants.

  • Systems Maintenance & Upgrades

Keeping the business humming along for over 800 daily users by closing tickets, implementing new HL7 interfaces, performing industry required updates to workflows like the ICD-9 ICD-10 transition, and keeping up to date with the latest system upgrades was par for the course as part of this small team at YAI. I was able to learn the ins-outs of the clinics we supported and got the sense that I too was having a positive-impact on a patient population suffering from Intellectual Developmental Disabilities that is in desperate need of care.
